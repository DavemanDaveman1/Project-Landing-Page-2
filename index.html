The Founding Fathers of the United States

The Founding Fathers of the United States were a group American revolutionary leaders who believed a certain set of principles, and united the thirteen colonies, and took on a war for independence against Great Britain. There were 7 most notable and key Founding Fathers were known as George Washington, John Adams, Thomas Jefferson, James Madison, Benjamin Franklin, Alexander Hamilton, and John Jay.

The First Four U.S. Presidents

George Washington (1789 - 1797)

John Adams (1797 - 1801)

Thomas Jefferson (1801 - 1809)

James Madison (1809 - 1817)
